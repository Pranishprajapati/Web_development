About Futsal Court

Futsal, a fast-paced variation of soccer, is gaining immense popularity worldwide. Played on a smaller, indoor pitch, futsal demands a high-quality playing surface that enhances performance and player safety. Traditional flooring solutions, like hardwood or concrete, can pose significant challenges, including maintenance difficulties and increased risk of injuries. This is where artificial turf solutions from Kaya Sports come into play, offering durability, low maintenance, and optimal playing conditions.

At Kaya Sports, we understand the unique requirements of futsal courts. Our Artificial turf solutions are specifically designed to meet the high-intensity demands of the game. In addition to our premium artificial turf, we also use top-quality materials like PVC 7mm and SPU (Silicon PU) to create futsal courts that meet international standards. These materials provide excellent durability, shock absorption, and slip resistance, enhancing the overall playing experience.

Size of Futsal Court

The size of Futsal is varies with a type of 5A and 7A Sides.

  • 5-A-Side (5A): The traditional format with five players per team, including the goalkeeper. This is the most common form of futsal.
  • 7-A-Side (7A): This format has seven players per team, including the goalkeeper, and is played on a slightly larger field compared to the 5A format.

Construction Process of Futsal Court

Planning and Design

Determine indoor or outdoor setup. Finalize court dimensions and choose surface type—artificial grass for outdoor or SPU flooring for indoor use.

Site Preparation

Excavate and level the site. Construct a compacted sub-base with proper slope and drainage to maintain surface stability and durability.

Surface Installation

Install the selected playing surface. For artificial turf, lay shockpad, turf, and apply infill. For SPU, apply cushioning and wear layers for a seamless, slip-resistant finish.

Finishing

Set up goalposts, court markings, and perimeter netting or fencing. Add lighting if needed. Conduct final inspection and provide maintenance instructions.
